Computer Aided Drug Design & Development (CADDD)

Ion Science is pioneer in offering dedicated Computational Chemistry services to worldwide pharmaceutical companies. We offer services truly scientific research and development..

In phase-I we started offering services - Ligand Based Drug Designing to serve our customers.
Computer Aided Drug Design (CADD)  is a method based on computer chemistry to design and optimize lead compounds through computer simulation, calculation and estimation of the relationship between the drug and the acceptor biomacromolecule.

CADD can be divided into receptor-based or structure-based drug design, SBDD and Ligand-based drug design, LBDD. Here is the relationship between the two methods. 


Ligand Based Drug Designing

Ligand Based Drug Designing is also called knowledge-based approach for lead identification, it is a very efficiebnt way of developing novel chemical matter using Ligand-based computational tools to design new scaffold or molecular fragments. The Ligand-based tool is effective when the reliable protein structure is absent. The known bioactive, a potent molecule is used to derive a pharmacophore model that defines the minimum neccessary structural activity relationship (QSAR), in which a correlation between the calculated properties of moleculesand their experimentaly determined biological activity, may be derived.

  • Potency and ADMET properties of a new compounds are predicted with experimental proof and chemoinformatics models
  • Conformational structure predicted, its stable geometrical structure, by using Qm calculation and conformational analysis by MD.
  • Diverse scaffold hopping approaches help to generate valuable ideas of novel IPs and overcome udersirable properties associated with structural elements.
  • Pharmacophore models, 3D alignment
  • Combinatorial chemistry and in silico generation of new structures


2D QSAR

D-QSAR is a drug design approach that utilizes the overall structural properties of the molecule as parameters. It is applied to perform regression analysis on the physiological activity of the molecule, and establish a model of the correlation between chemical structure and physiological activity. 2D-QSAR models are used routinely during the process of optimization of a chemical series towards a candidate for clinical trials.

3D QSAR

Most of the molecular descriptors of 2D-QSAR are used to describe the changes between atomic chemical bonds. However, when these molecular descriptors are not enough to accurately describe the molecular structure, field and space factors need to be considered. Therefore, 3D-QSAR technology using three-dimensional structure is applied to more accurately describe molecular structure

ADMET Prediction

ADMET (absorption, distribution, metabolism, excretion and toxicity) pharmacokinetic prediction method is a very important approach in drug design and drug screening. Early studies on the properties of drugs in ADMET mainly focused on human-derived or humanized tissue functional proteins as drug targets.

Chemical space docking aims to predict actives based on their binding mode after a docking procedure with subsequent scoring of the complexes. Yet the source of the docked molecules is no longer an enumerated library but a combinatorial built-up 'space' of zillions of molecules.

Ligand-based Pharmacophore Modelling

A pharmacophore is defined as the ensemble of steric and electronic features that is necessary to ensure the optimal supramolecular interactions with a specific biological target structure. In general, a useful pharmacophore involves important information about functional groups that interact with the target, and the type of noncovalent interactions and interatomic distances between these functional groups/interactions.

Hit Identification

Hit identification is a powerful tool for discovering compounds with the desired activity against a fully validated target. Hit identification is an essential step of the drug discovery process, in which the identified small molecules will bind to the target, usually a protein. Scientists therefore can modify its function and improve bioactivity.

Molecular Dynamics Simulation

As a multi-body simulation technique, molecular dynamics simulation method mainly relies on computers to simulate the movement of molecules and atomic systems. The behaviour of the system over time can be investigated from a dynamic point of view by simulating the state of motion of molecules and atoms in a certain period of time.

Molecular Simulation

Molecular Simulation Molecular simulation is a method of using computers to simulate molecular structure and behavior with atomic-level molecular models, various physical and chemical properties of molecular systems can be obtained with this technology. It builds a set of models and algorithms based on experiments and basic principles to calculate reasonable molecular structures and molecular behaviors. Molecular simulation approach can simulate not only the static structure of molecules, but also the dynamic behavior of molecular systems.

Lead Optimization

Lead optimization is a complex and iterative process for identifying an improved drug lead with the goal of progressing to a preclinical candidate by altering the chemical structure of a confirmed hit. A diversity of properties such as target’s specificity, selectivity, pharmacodynamics, pharmacokinetics and toxicological properties are improved by modifying the chemical structures of compounds or biologics in lead optimization process.
