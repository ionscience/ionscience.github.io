Ligand-based Pharmacophore Modeling

One of the important ligand-based pharmacophore modeling apporaches 3D QSAR so going forward we must introduce to Pharmacophore. A pharmacophore is defined as the ensemble of steric and electronic features that is necessary to ensure the optimal supramolecular interactions with a specific biological target structure. In general, a useful pharmacophore involves important information about functional groups that interact with the target, and the type of noncovalent interactions and interatomic distances between these functional groups/interactions.

Ligand-based Pharmacophore Modeling Strategies

In order to create a ligand-based pharmacophore, structurally similar bioactive compounds have been taken overlaid in such a way that a maximum number of chemical features overlap geometrically involving rigid 2D or 3D structural representations or incorporate molecular flexibility to determine overlapping sites. Ion Science applies two approaches to generate a ligand-based pharmacophore model:


 a) Create anf study the conformations according to computaions and study interactions


 b) Explore conformations by changing molecule coordinates as needed by the alignment algorithm

Our Services

    At Ion Science impliments multiple methods to perform high-qualtiy pharmacophore modeling by employing pharmacophore generation algorithm:

  • Superimposing active compounds to create a pharmacophore

    • 1. We use Guassian functions to align molecules either a point-based or property-based technique.


    2. Sample Conformational space can be tested by Rigid, flexible, and semiflexible method.


    3. Molecular dynamics is our best tool to perform conformational search during the alignment process.


    4. We also use active analog approach to limit the exploration of conformational space


  • Pharmacophore feature extraction

    • In general, hydrogen bond acceptors and donors, acidic and basic groups, aliphatic hydrophobic moieties, and aromatic hydrophobic moieties are the most common features used to define pharmacophore maps. Moreover, features are commonly implemented as spheres with a certain tolerance radius for pharmacophore matching.


  • Software Platform Pharmacophore mapping

    • Ion Science tests and uses various software platforms and algorithms for ligand-based pharmacophore building and apply different approaches to molecular alignment, flexibility, and feature extraction


    1. Phase: It supports a tree-based partitioning algorithm and an RMS deviation-based scoring function that considers the volume of heavy atom overlap. In addition, a Monte Carlo or torsional search is applied to incorporate molecular flexibility.


    2. Catalyst: It uses two algorithms for pharmacophore construction, and performs alignment and feature extraction by identifying common chemical features arranged in certain positions in three-dimensional space.


    3. DISCO: It fully considers multiple conformations and uses a clique-detection algorithm for scoring alignments.


    4. GASP: It uses a genetic algorithm with iterative generations of the best models for pharmacophore construction. Flexibility can be handled during the alignment process using random rotations and translations, and conformations are optimized by fitting them to similarity constraints and weighing the conformations.
