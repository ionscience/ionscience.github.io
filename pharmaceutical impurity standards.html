Pharmaceutical Impurity Standard

The impurities in Pharmaceuticals are unwanted chemicals that remain with the active pharmaceutical ingradients (APIs) or develope during formulation oe upon aging of both API and formulation. The presence of these unwanted chemicals even in trace amount may influence the efficacy and safety of pharmaceutical product. The control of impurities is a important task as per the regulatory athorities and norms.

Types of reference-standard Materials

Reference-standard material can be broadly categarised as:

  • Assay - used to determine potency for Active Pharmaceutical ingradients (APIs)and salts
  • Degradation product - used to identify and posibily to quantitate degradation products
  • Process impurities - used to identify and possibily quantitate process related compounds
  • Resolution - Resolution - used to determine asaay performance or impurity method
  • Metabolites - used to identify and possibily to quantitate substances generated through a Metabolite process

Organic Impurities

One of the challenging part of developing a most accurate method, since, the impurities are charastically related to parent compound and diffreent degradation pathways can resulted into different impurities.

Impurities that arises from the raw materials, synthesis, purification and storage require careful consideration because they may not produce detector responses that are related to reference-standard material. impurities must be isolated and identified so that an appropriate reference standard can be used, or relative response factor determined.
